#
# This is a RB2300 AUX outputs mapping. O means AUX output. e.g. O4 = AUX4
# ">" represents driving direction "A"
# "<" represents opposite to driving direction "B"
# Example: O4:F0< means light output no. 4 will light up, when F0 function is on and in the driving direction "A"
#
# Convention:
# F0 - Always Pc1
# F8 - Always driver's cabin
# F6 - Always shunting
# F5 - Always Pc2
# F7 - Always Pc5
#

# Pc1, two front white lights
O4:F0<
O7:F0<
O1:F0>
O6:F0>

# Pc1, white headlight above the two white headlights of the locomotive each front
O12:F0<
O5:F0>

# Drivers cabin
O11:F8<
O10:F8>

# Pc5 (train end signal - two red lights at the locomotive side opposite to driving direction)
O2:F7>
O9:F7>
O8:F7<
O3:F7<

# Pc2 (driving on invalid track, when two or more rails on the way. On locomotive front: 1x head light + left red light + right white light on the driver position)
O12:F5<
O5:F5>

O4:F5<
O9:F5<

O1:F5>
O3:F5>

# Tb1 (shunting light. One white light on both front and back of the locomotive (opposite driving direction), always on right side from perspective of driving direction - where driver seat is placed)
O4:F6<
O1:F6<

O7:F6>
O6:F6>
